This image is derived from a Lidar survey and shows the topography of the lower town and river valley. The feature running across the image is the railway line, running into the tunnel east of the station!                                                    ;                           ;                           ;                           & The mid-grey band flanking the Avon is a flat terrace formed by the river over thousands of years. This is typical of our river valleys, and such terraces attracted settlements of all periods.
Prof.David Hinton has noted that the church is located on the very edge of the terrace. It's known that a monastery was established in Bradford around AD 650-700 and the Saxon settlement may have grown up around it. The Saxon church dates from around AD1000 and its position suggests that much of the ground was already occupied.
